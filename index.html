What makes me a credible source when it comes to advice and knowledge about Japan?

Despite being born and raised in San Diego, California, my two fully Japanese immigrant parents made sure I learned Japanese and stayed in touch with my cultural heritage. Every Saturday for 11 years, I attended a supplementary Japanese school in San Diego called Minato Gakuen, where we learned a wide variety of subjects from Japanese language, history, geography and politics, in addition to the more universal math, science, etc. Instruction was only in Japanese and combined with the Japanese spoken at home, I am blessed to say that I have retained fluency of the language. I also lived in Japan for 2 years prior to entering first grade, when my family moved to Japan before eventually moving back.

I have also been invited to speak as a representative of my class on multiple occasions throughout the years, most recently when I gave a speech at Balboa Park's Japanese Friendship Garden here in San Diego for our coming of age ceremony, known as Seijinshiki (成人式). Why does this page exist?

This website was born out of my passion for Japan and my expertise on its culture. This project aims to cater to the ever-growing demand of tourists hoping to visit Japan, providing them with information so that they can enjoy an awesome and unforgettable trip.
